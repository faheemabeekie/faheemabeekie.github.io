Seeing the differences:

Cat Breed Description Traits Physical Appearance
Siamese The Siamese cat is a breed of domestic short-haired cats that originated in Thailand. Kittens are born white or cream coloured and later develop the dark points (ears, face, legs, and tail) characteristic of the breed.
  • Smart
  • Needy
  • Talkative
Bengal The Bengal is a sleek, muscular cat with a wild appearance, enhanced by the bold marbling and spotting on their thick, luxurious coat.
  • Curious
  • Active
  • Vocal
Maine Coon The Maine Coon is a solid, rugged cat that is well-proportioned and balanced despite their massive size. They have muscular, long, rectangular bodies with broad chests. Their large, round paws have tufts of hair sticking up from the toes.
  • Easy to train
  • Loyal
  • Social
Munchkin The Munchkin is a medium-sized cat that may appear smaller because of their short legs. Besides the leg length, the Munchkin is a very normal-looking cat, bearing characteristics of their particular breeding lineage. Generally speaking, the Munchkin's coat is soft and plush, ranging in length from short to long.
  • Playful
  • Active
  • Affectionate
Spyhnx The Sphynx cat’s appearance is truly distinctive. These felines are known for being hairless, with a wrinkled skin and a muscular build. They have prominent cheekbones and large, wide-set ears that contribute to their unique look.
  • Playful
  • Energetic
  • Clingy
